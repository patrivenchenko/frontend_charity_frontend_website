The Value Of Community Service

Giving back to society is in fact vague and misty in nature. There’s so much that we procure from society. There are so many strata of people who make up the whole population of a nation. But they are different from each other. Some are affluent and some are mere stragglers of survival. In many countries, there are masses who don’t even have excess to basic day-to-day requirements. There are also people who have so much in excess, that the amounts if distributed, would solve many problems plaguing most areas around the world. “Without community service, we would not have a strong quality of life. Give to him who asks of you, and do not turn away.

But, there comes minimum or negligible effort from us as a whole to emancipate at least the smallest fraction of the problem. There are scriptures of all the faiths that actually teach us to give and share. But perhaps in this fast, mechanical world, the words ‘give’ and ‘share’ have been drooped to nothingness. We shall browse through some of wise words from famous stall-wards of society who have tried to inculcate the goodness of benevolence in us. These words, which we commonly call quotes, are motivational to take up an action which has been neglected since long, really long. “But whoever has the world’s goods, and sees his brother in need. It’s important to the person who serves as well as the recipient. It’s the way in which we ourselves grow and develop.” “Never doubt that a small group of thoughtful committed citizens can change the world: indeed, it’s the only thing that ever has.” “Is it not to divide your bread with the hungry And bring the homeless poor into the house; When you see the naked, to cover him; And not to hide yourself from your own flesh?”. “Wealth is not new. Neither is charity. But the idea of using private wealth imaginatively, constructively, and systematically to attack the fundamental problems of mankind is new.” “I will continue to distribute blankets, sleeping bags, warm clothing and food on a regular basis, in the hope that my modest efforts will give some comfort to those people we are able to help.” “There is a natural law, a Divine law, that obliges you and me to relieve the suffering, the distressed and the destitute.
